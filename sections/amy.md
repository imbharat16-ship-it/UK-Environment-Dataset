### Amy (Phase 2 · Confirm)

**Profile:** Originally from an eastern European country (grew up in a household shaped by Soviet-era food scarcity via her mother). Lives in military housing in Oxfordshire, two-person household (husband frequently away for work); no children, one dog. Works for an organisation in Oxfordshire. Passionate about plastic packaging reduction; emotionally expressive about it ("frustration, sadness, anger, confusion"). Primary shopper; shops mainly at Lidl (local, cheap) and occasionally Waitrose. Uses Oddbox fortnightly veg-rescue box. Stopped using clingfilm several years ago; replaced with silicone stretch lids. Collects flexible plastics in a dedicated bag to take to supermarket carrier-bag bins. Has an organic food waste caddy collected weekly. 5 diary batches.

**Themes:**

- **Background / identity:** Grew up in eastern Europe; mum was a housewife with a Soviet-era "refuse to waste" ethos (everything cooked from scratch, food respected). - **Emotional response to packaging:** Describes plastic packaging as "ridiculous," "sad," "crazy," "madness." Emotional about the systemic failure — individual households generating bags of non-recyclable plastic. Previously took flexible plastics back to Tesco (before store recycling was mainstream); felt like "a criminal" doing it.
- **Disposal / recycling setup:** Three indoor bins (general, recycling, organic food waste caddy with compostable liners); large black bin and green recycling bin kerbside (alternate fortnightly); food waste caddy collected weekly. Avocados, bananas, spinach, rocket, asparagus, salad portions individually plastic-wrapped despite not needing it. Finds single-serving pre-portioned items (cut onion, individual salad servings) symptomatic of societal laziness.
- **No clingfilm:** Stopped buying it years ago; uses a set of silicone stretch lids purchased on Amazon. Transition was easy; now finds visiting her mum and seeing clingfilm everywhere uncomfortable. Gave her mum a set of lids but suspects they are unused.
- **Reuse of containers:** Cupboard full of takeaway containers, peanut-butter jars, yoghurt pots kept for food storage and microwave reheating. Wonders about health implications of repeated microwave use but admits she may be avoiding the answer for convenience.
