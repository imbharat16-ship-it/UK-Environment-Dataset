### Sophia (Phase 3 · Extend)

**Profile:** Married woman in Lancaster area; lives with husband (a competitive masters-level runner who represents Great Britain) and 8-year-old son in a semi-detached, 3-bedroom rented house. Works part-time from home (Tue, Wed, alternate Mon). The whole family runs regularly; son plays football coached by husband. New puppy acquired before study start. Shops primarily at Aldi (plus occasional Asda/Lidl top-up). Manages all meal planning, cooking, and most food preparation; describes past struggles with food and exercise obsession that shape her current approach to portion control and packaging choices.

#### Interview 1 (Step 0) — 13,007 words · 66,747 characters

**Themes:**
- **Household/home:** Semi-detached, 3-bed house; small galley kitchen treated purely as functional space. Husband commutes to a university; son has school hot lunches four days and packed lunch one day (school requires packaging returned). Significant carpet renovation during study Week 1.
- **Routines:** Exercise-structured week — family runs together most days; son has football training and matches. Evening meals taken together around 6.30 pm. Sophia does all meal planning (Wednesday night cupboard audit + list) and almost all cooking. No takeaways on school nights.
- **Shopping:** Weekly Aldi shop (~30 min, list-led, bags for life); occasionally tops up at Asda for branded items son prefers. Asks family for meal ideas Wednesday evening; plans 5–6 definite meals, wings the rest. Checks for in-season Aldi specials (59p pineapple) and reduced items.
- **Food/preparation:** Cooks family meals from a portfolio: pesto chicken pasta, Bolognese gnocchi, enchiladas, moussaka, wrap pizzas, "loose take on a roast." Freezes raw chicken in sandwich bags to save freezer space; separates pork belly portions similarly. Keeps frozen veg, fruit, and bread to reduce waste. Pre-mades mash and frozen roast potatoes used for convenience.
- **Plastic packaging:** Recycling determined first by Aldi's printed "tray recycle / film don't recycle" labelling, then by triangle numbers. Chicken and pork trays recycled; thin films, crisp packets, popcorn bags, individual sweet wrappers all go to general waste. Husband handles all physical recycling.
- **Disposal/recycling:** Recycling stacked by back door; husband takes to outdoor boxes daily. Cardboard broken flat. Three outdoor boxes (cardboard/paper; plastic + glass + tins). Fortnightly kerbside, recently changed to alternating weeks. Rinses pasta sauce jars (often adding a splash of water to the cooking); mum's teaching.
- **Reuse:** Sandwich bags reused for son's sweets and puppy treats. Cottage cheese and Tupperware for leftovers. Carries own bottles, Tupperwares, and picnic food on outings.
- **Holiday:** Christmas in Lanzarote; airline water bottles filled before security; recycled hotel large water bottles in external crates; all other waste in one bin bag (no choice). Post-return normal shop immediately.

**Behavioral lenses:** Identity (runner/athlete, managing past food/exercise obsession), Pricing/value (Aldi loyalty, in-season specials), Convenience tradeoff (frozen mash and veg vs. scratch cooking), Food waste sensitivity (plans meals to use up ingredients across days), Recycling diligence (label-led, Aldi reliable guide), Occasion (school night vs. weekend "fakeaway"), Attitude-behavior gap (wants more loose produce but Aldi stocks only packaged).
