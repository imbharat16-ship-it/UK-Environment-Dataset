# Gemma (Phase 2 · Confirm)

### Gemma (Phase 2 · Confirm)

**Profile:** American (from the US), 33; has lived in the UK for approximately 10 years. Just completed a PhD (autumn 2021) and is now working three days a week at an Oxford bookshop while applying for post-doctoral positions and doing part-time research and teaching. Lodger in a house owned by a retired-teacher couple; shares a kitchen, living room, and garden with the couple and one other lodger; has a mini fridge (shared with the other lodger), two kitchen cupboards, and a half-freezer. Walks or cycles to the shop (aware of bag weight). Diet is habitual and simple while lodging; eats a lot of fresh salad, fruit, bread, and snacks; reducing meat consumption mainly for environmental reasons. Keepcup user (kept at work). Saves bread bags and resealable nut bags for reuse; has a reusable tin container for snacks; stores nuts in glass jam jars; silicone pouches gifted by cousin. No car; travels by train and bus for academic work. Mum in the US sends food parcels 2–3 times a year.

#### Interview 1 (Step 0) — 9,260 words · 45,994 characters

**Themes:**
- **Household/home:** Lodger in a retired couple's Oxford house; one other lodger; shares kitchen and living spaces; mini fridge with half-freezer shared with other lodger; two personal cupboards (full and messy due to limited space); garden with compost. Limited storage shapes shopping frequency and food choices.
- **Routines:** Bookshop shifts Mon/Fri/Sat; rest of week for job applications and research. Walks (~2.5 miles) or cycles to shop; acutely aware of bag weight. Eats at predictable times when lodging: toast and black coffee for breakfast every morning; sandwich and snacks for work lunches; salad with protein or roasted veg for dinner.
- **Food/diet:** Reducing meat for environmental reasons and body signals; occasional salmon (a few times a month), haloumi, sweet potato and chickpea curry; mostly plant-forward. "Creature of habit" — decision fatigue means she sticks to what works. Grazes on nuts, apples, pretzels, chocolate, granola. Mum in the US health-conscious, which rubbed off.
- **Shopping:** No fixed weekly shop; goes every other day or pops in for single items; works across the street from a grocery store. Buys multiples when something is on offer (granola, chocolate). Limited fridge space means she cannot do a large weekly shop.
- **Recycling setup:** Two cannisters under the sink (general waste and mixed recycling); landlords' compost bin in the garden for food waste; rolls bins to kerbside for collection. Oxford recycles mixed (glass, cardboard, paper, plastic pots/punnets); harder plastic film goes to black bin.
- **Recycling behaviour:** Checks labels; defaults hard plastics (pots, punnets) to recycling and film/clingfilm-type plastic to bin. If no label on hard plastic, assumes recyclable; aware this is a generalisation. Previously lived in Lancaster where fewer plastics were accepted.
- **Reuse:** Saves bread bags for taking food to work (lighter than Tupperware; beeswax wrap also used but comes unfolded in bag); keeps resealable nut bags (sturdier zip-lock); glass jam jars for nuts; tin container for snacks; carrier bags kept and reused for the recycling run.
- **Plastic food packaging views:** Tries to avoid plastic; often convenience, cost, or necessity wins. Buys pre-made salads (M&S, Waitrose) when travelling or at work; aware these generate plastic she'd rather avoid.

**Behavioral lenses:** Convenience tradeoff (proximity to work shop; weight of shopping bag; lodger space constraints), Identity (health-conscious upbringing; reducing meat for environment; US to UK transition), Recycling diligence (checks labels; Oxford vs Lancaster comparison; film vs hard plastic rule), Reuse tendency (bread bags; nut bags; glass jars; beeswax wrap), Pricing/value (buys multiples on offer; aware of bag-weight cost), Food waste sensitivity (mum's health-conscious influence; cooking curry to last several nights).
